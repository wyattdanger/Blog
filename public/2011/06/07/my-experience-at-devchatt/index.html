My Experience at Dev[Chatt]

Jun 7th, 2011

I had the opportunity to present on front-end Web performance optimization at DevChatt this past weekend. It was a great small conference, with most panels covering topics related to front-end engineering or infrastructure.

Picking a topic was hard. This was my first conference speaking opportunity. After considering the work I’ve focused on in the past year, I decided that performance is something I’ve learned much about.

My slides are available here in HTML. To build the presentation I used Landslide, open-source software which parses a Markdown file and turns it into a full-featured HTML5 slideshow.

Presentation I saw

  • Chris Rimondi on IPV6
  • Timmy Willison of jQuery core on attr/prop in JQuery 1.6
  • Daniel Ryan on CSS3 (slides)
  • Donald Sayers on Paywall.me
  • Mike Dirolf on MongoDB
